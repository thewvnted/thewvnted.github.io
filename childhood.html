My Childhood

Early memories and formative experiences that shaped who I am today

Early Years

I was born and raised in Malindi, Old Town. From a young age, I was always curious about the world around me. My parents often tell stories about how I would constantly ask "why" about everything I encountered.

Age 5: First Day of School

I remember feeling both excited and nervous on my first day of kindergarten. My teacher, Mrs. Mwanasiti, made learning fun and helped me develop a love for reading.

Age 8: Discovering Art

My grandmother gave me my first set of watercolor paints. I spent hours creating colorful paintings.

Age 13: First Computer

My family got our first computer, and I was fascinated by this magical machine. I spent countless hours exploring what it could do.

Age 15: Moving to a New Town

My family moved to a new town, which was challenging at first. But this experience taught me resilience and how to adapt to new environments and make new friends.

Family Influence

My family played a crucial role in shaping my values and interests. My mother introduced me to creative writing, while my dad taught me the importance of having principles in life.

Some of my fondest childhood memories include:

  • Family vacations to Watamu
  • Weekly game nights with my family
  • Helping my grandparents in their garden
  • December trips with my cousins

Formative Experiences

Several key experiences during my childhood significantly influenced my personality and future direction:

Overcoming Challenges

When I was 10, I struggled with public speaking. Working through this taught me perseverance and problem-solving skills that I still use today.

Discovering Passions

My first computer class opened up a new world for me and helped me discover my love for technology as a whole.

Building Relationships

My friendship with Ian, my childhood best friend taught me the value of loyalty, empathy, and supporting others through good times and bad. 18 years down the line and we're still as close as ever.
